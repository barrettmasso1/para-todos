# Town Hall Facilitator Guide — First Community Session
## For: Sarai Torres + Barrett Masso
## Date: Thursday 2026-05-28 or Friday 2026-05-29 (TBD)

This is the single most important document of the launch. The first town hall sets the tone for everything that follows. Optimize for **listening signals**, not advocacy signals. If attendees leave saying "I felt heard," you win — regardless of what they actually feel about the idea.

---

## Pre-session checklist (24h before)

- [ ] Venue confirmed + capacity ~30–50 chairs arranged in arc (not rows facing podium — too formal)
- [ ] Printed one-pager handouts (50–100 copies, bilingual)
- [ ] Projector + laptop tested with deck v2 loaded in offline mode (don't rely on venue WiFi)
- [ ] Two flip charts + markers (one Spanish, one English) for live note-capture
- [ ] Sign-in sheet (paper backup to landing page form) with: name, email, WhatsApp, community, language preference
- [ ] Water + simple refreshments (cafecito + agua) — signals hospitality, lowers tension
- [ ] Translator identified if mixed audience expected (community member volunteer ideal, NOT a hired translator who feels external)
- [ ] Photographer (with attendee consent only) — for landing page social proof later
- [ ] Recording (audio only, with consent) — for honest summary publication; offer attendees to opt-out
- [ ] One trusted community elder or respected resident introduced to open the room (not Sarai/Barrett — they convene, but someone else opens)

---

## Room setup philosophy

- **No podium.** Sarai + Barrett sit at the front in chairs, not stand at a lectern.
- **Arc seating, not rows.** Signals conversation, not presentation.
- **Spanish first language of the room.** Even if attendees skew English-speaking, default the opening, headers, and structural language to Spanish. English translations follow.
- **No press at first session.** Even if a journalist asks, politely defer to a later session. First sessions need privacy for honest pushback.

## Agenda (90 minutes total)

### 0:00 — 0:10 — Welcome (10 min)

**Who speaks:** Local community elder or respected resident introduces Sarai + Barrett. NOT Sarai/Barrett opening cold.

**Opening script (in Spanish, English right after):**

> "Gracias a todos por venir. Esta es una conversación, no una reunión informativa. Vamos a compartir lo que sabemos, lo que no sabemos, y queremos escuchar lo que ustedes piensan. No tenemos respuestas — tenemos preguntas que queremos hacer juntos.
>
> Reglas del espacio:
> 1. Cualquiera puede preguntar cualquier cosa.
> 2. No vamos a defendernos — vamos a escuchar.
> 3. Lo que se dice aquí queda aquí, salvo lo que cada quien quiera compartir.
> 4. Si alguien dice 'no estoy de acuerdo', ese es el momento más valioso de la noche — porque ahí aprendemos."

> "Thank you all for coming. This is a conversation, not an informational meeting. We're going to share what we know, what we don't know, and we want to hear what you think. We don't have answers — we have questions we want to ask together.
>
> Room rules:
> 1. Anyone can ask anything.
> 2. We won't get defensive — we'll listen.
> 3. What's said here stays here, except what each person chooses to share.
> 4. If someone says 'I disagree,' that's the most valuable moment of the night — because that's where we learn."

### 0:10 — 0:25 — The Why (15 min)

**Who speaks:** Sarai (in Spanish primarily), with Barrett adding context in English where helpful.

**Walk through Slides 1–3 of deck v2:**
- Slide 1: Why this conversation, why now
- Slide 2: The current reality (growth + pressures)
- Slide 3: What this looks like in daily life (water/power/development vignettes)

**Key facilitation moves:**
- After each vignette, pause 5 seconds. Let it land.
- Then ask: *"¿Alguien ha vivido algo así? / Has anyone experienced something like this?"*
- Capture responses on flip chart. Don't reply yet. Just listen + record.

### 0:25 — 0:40 — The What (15 min)

**Walk through Slides 4–6:**
- Slide 4: How decisions are made today (org structure)
- Slide 5: Our region, visualized (map — show two scenarios honestly)
- Slide 6: Two honest futures (side-by-side comparison)

**Critical facilitation move on Slide 5 (map):**
- Show the Pacific-coast-only scenario AND mention the East Cape scenario
- Say explicitly: *"Esto no está decidido. Es por estudiar. La comunidad decide qué territorio tiene sentido."*
- *"This is not decided. It's to be studied. The community decides what territory makes sense."*

**Critical facilitation move on Slide 6 (two futures):**
- DO NOT push Scenario B. Acknowledge Scenario A (stay in La Paz) as legitimate.
- Say: *"Hay buenas razones para cualquiera de las dos. No estamos aquí para venderles una respuesta."*
- *"There are good reasons for either path. We're not here to sell you an answer."*

### 0:40 — 0:55 — The How (15 min)

**Walk through Slides 7–9:**
- Slide 7: What the law actually allows (3 gates)
- Slide 8: What it would cost, transparently (~$10M, stage-gated, open books, community veto)
- Slide 9: Who's behind this (Sarai + Barrett + more to come)

**Critical facilitation move on Slide 8 (cost):**
- Show the number openly. ~$10M MXN over 20 months.
- Acknowledge: *"Es mucho dinero. Por eso es por etapas, y por eso queremos que cada quien apoye con lo que pueda — 50 pesos o 500,000."*
- *"It's a lot of money. That's why it's staged, and that's why we want everyone to support with whatever they can — 50 pesos or 500,000."*

**Critical facilitation move on Slide 9 (who):**
- Don't make this about credentials. Make it about why-care.
- Sarai's 30-second story: why she cares as a longtime resident.
- Barrett's 30-second story: why he cares as a resident + business owner.
- Then: *"El comité crecerá. No estamos solos en preguntar esto."*
- *"The committee will grow. We're not alone in asking this."*

### 0:55 — 1:30 — Listening session (35 min — the most important part)

**This is the entire purpose of the night. Don't shortchange it.**

**Open the floor with 5 prompts (deck Slide 11 has them):**

1. ¿Cómo ven el futuro de nuestra región?
   *How do you see the future of our region?*

2. ¿Cuáles son sus mayores preocupaciones sobre el crecimiento?
   *What are your biggest concerns about growth?*

3. ¿Creen que el gobierno local podría hacer la diferencia?
   *Do you think local governance could make a difference?*

4. ¿Qué necesitarían para sentirse seguros con cualquier cambio grande?
   *What would you need to feel confident in any major change?*

5. ¿Quién más debería ser parte de esta conversación?
   *Who else should be part of this conversation?*

**Facilitation rules during listening:**

- **No defensiveness.** If someone says "this is a developer land grab," DO NOT argue. Say: *"Gracias por la honestidad. Cuéntanos más — ¿qué te hace pensar eso?"* / "Thank you for the honesty. Tell us more — what makes you think that?"
- **No promises.** If someone says "but will my taxes go up?" — say: *"Esa es la pregunta correcta. La verdad es que no lo sabemos todavía porque eso lo decidiría un nuevo cabildo, si es que llegamos ahí. Lo que sí podemos decir es: los impuestos federales y estatales no cambian."*
- **No predictions.** If someone says "do you think it will pass?" — say: *"No lo sé. Por eso preguntamos primero, antes de cualquier decisión."*
- **Capture verbatim.** Have a third person (Founding Committee future member, or trusted note-taker) write key quotes on flip chart.
- **Embrace the skeptics.** Thank them loudly when they push back. *"Justo por eso estamos aquí — para escuchar este tipo de inquietudes."*
- **Manage dominant voices.** If one person is monopolizing, say: *"Gracias [Nombre], muy buena observación. ¿Quién más quiere agregar?"*

---

### 1:25 — 1:35 — Close (10 min)

**Who speaks:** Barrett wraps in Spanish + English.

**Closing script:**

> "Gracias. Lo que hemos escuchado hoy lo vamos a publicar — un resumen anónimo de los temas y preocupaciones — para que las próximas sesiones sigan construyendo sobre esto, no repitan desde cero.
>
> La próxima sesión es [fecha], en [lugar]. Por favor inviten a sus vecinos, especialmente a los que aún no han escuchado de esto.
>
> Si quieren mantenerse informados, regístrense en la mesa de entrada o en paratodos.mx/futuro. Si quieren ayudar — como voluntarios o con un aporte económico — también ahí pueden hacerlo.
>
> Y si después de esta noche piensan 'no, esto no es para nosotros' — esa también es una respuesta legítima, y queremos escucharla."

> "Thank you. What we've heard tonight we'll publish — an anonymous summary of the themes and concerns — so the next sessions can build on this, not start from scratch.
>
> The next session is [date], at [venue]. Please invite your neighbors, especially those who haven't heard about this yet.
>
> If you want to stay informed, sign up at the entrance table or at paratodos.mx/futuro. If you want to help — as a volunteer or with a financial contribution — you can do that there too.
>
> And if after tonight you think 'no, this isn't for us' — that's also a legitimate answer, and we want to hear it."

**Do NOT make a hard donation ask at the close.** Mention the landing page exists, leave handouts, and let people self-select. First session is about establishing trust, not capturing dollars.

## Red flags during the session — what to do

| Red flag | Response |
|---|---|
| Attendee accuses Barrett of being a developer/outsider pushing agenda | Don't get defensive. *"Soy residente, soy parte de esta comunidad, y entiendo la pregunta. Hablemos de qué te haría sentir que esto es legítimo."* |
| Multiple attendees pushing back on costs ("waste of money") | Acknowledge fully. *"Es justo. Por eso es por etapas — si la Etapa I no convence a la comunidad, no seguimos. No se gastan los $10M de un solo golpe."* |
| Conspiracy framing ("you're working with the cartel/government/etc.") | Don't argue. *"Entiendo que hay desconfianza. ¿Qué evidencia te haría sentir que es lo que decimos que es?"* Listen, then move on. |
| Strong support that wants to push too fast ("let's just sign petitions tonight!") | Slow it down. *"Aprecio el entusiasmo. Pero queremos que esto se haga bien — y eso significa primero escuchar, después estudiar, después actuar. Nada se firma esta noche."* |
| Hostile interruption / disruption | Hold the room. *"Te escuchamos. Toma 30 segundos. Después seguimos con la siguiente pregunta."* If escalating, take a 5-minute break. |
| Someone asks for hard numbers we don't have | Be honest. *"No lo sabemos todavía — eso es parte de lo que el estudio de Etapa I va a determinar. Lo que sí podemos decir es [closest known fact]."* |

## Tone reminders (for both facilitators)

- **You are the host of someone else's house.** This isn't your conversation to control. You opened it for the community.
- **Skeptics are gifts.** Every pushback teaches you what to fix in the deck or process. Welcome them publicly.
- **Don't sell.** This is not a pitch. It's an invitation to think together.
- **Spanish first, always.** Even if you're more comfortable in English, default to Spanish. It signals respect.
- **Stay calm even if attacked.** Defensive = guilty in this context. Calm = trustworthy.
- **End on a question, not a statement.** Last words from facilitators should be a question to the room, not a closing argument.

---

## What "success" looks like for the first session

✅ **30+ attendees** representing 3+ communities
✅ **At least 5 distinct sectors** represented (agriculture, tourism, hospitality, professional services, education, fishing, ranchero, etc.)
✅ **At least 2 substantive concerns** voiced openly (not just polite nodding)
✅ **At least 5 sign-ups** for future sessions
✅ **At least 1 person** asks to join the Founding Committee
✅ **No press, no recordings leaked** that misrepresent the session
✅ **Anonymized summary published within 48h**

🚫 **Failure modes:**
- < 15 attendees → bad outreach, fix before next session
- Echo chamber (only supporters present) → outreach to skeptical communities aggressively before next session
- Hard donation ask made during session → reset expectations for next session
- Sarai or Barrett got defensive → debrief together, role-play challenging Qs before next session
- No one pushed back → people held back. Build smaller-group breakout sessions to surface honest concerns
