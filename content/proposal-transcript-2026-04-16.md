## Legal pathway cited (Pages 5–6)

### Constitución Política del Estado de Baja California Sur

- **Article 64.XXXV:** State Congress has the power to create or abolish Municipalities and reform political division of the State by **two-thirds vote of total Deputies.**
- **Article 122 (Creation, Suppression and Association of Municipalities)** — requires Congress approval AND the following 8 elements:
  - I. Convenient to satisfy inhabitants' needs
  - II. Territory surface area sufficient for current needs + future development
  - III. Demonstration before Congress of sufficient elements for political existence
  - IV. **Population not less than 5,000 inhabitants**
  - V. **Cabecera (seat) community with more than 3,000 inhabitants**
  - VI. Cabecera city has adequate public services for its population
  - VII. **Prior consultation with affected existing Municipal Councils (i.e., La Paz cabildo)** must be heard
  - VIII. **At least two-thirds of citizens of the proposed territory express agreement in a plebiscite**

### Ley de Participación Ciudadana para el Estado de BCS

- **Article 4.I:** Plebiscite is a recognized mechanism of Citizen Participation
- **Article 14:** Plebiscite definition — public consultation for citizens to express affirmative/negative opinion on Executive Power / Municipal Council acts considered transcendental, including formation/suppression/merger of Municipalities
- **Article 16.III:** Citizen requests for formation of new municipality CAN be submitted to plebiscite
- **Article 17.IV:** Citizens (men and women) of the State CAN request a plebiscite
- **Article 19.III:** Citizen request must be backed by **at least 33% of registered voters in the Voter Roll (Lista Nominal de Electores)** of the affected Municipality(ies)
- **Article 19.IV:** Must attach full names, signatures, and voter ID keys (clave de elector) of each supporting citizen
- **Article 31:** Once the Institute declares the request admissible, it has 15 calendar days to issue the call, and the plebiscite must be held within 90 calendar days after the call. Call must be published in Official Gazette + 2 publications in major newspaper + dissemination through mass media.

### Three hard numeric gates derived from the law

1. **33% of the Voter Roll signatures collected** to file the plebiscite request (Vita's quote: 3,500 signatures)
2. **2/3 of the plebiscite vote in favor** (per Article 122.VIII + Article 16.III)
3. **2/3 of the BCS State Congress deputies in favor** (per Article 64.XXXV)

All three thresholds must be hit for the new municipality to be created.
